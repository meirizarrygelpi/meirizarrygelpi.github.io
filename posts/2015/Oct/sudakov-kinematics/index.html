Sudakov Kinematics

Date Wed 14 October 2015 By M.E. Irizarry-Gelpí Category Physics Tags kinematics

Recently I learned a cool trick. It allows you to write a set of slow momenta in terms of null momenta. By slow momentum I mean an energy-momentum vector that describes a quantum that moves slower than light. By null momentum I mean an energy-momentum vector that describes a quantum that moves at the same speed as light. For concreteness, consider a 2-to-2 scattering process:

$$ A(p_{1}) + B(p_{2}) \longrightarrow C(p_{3}) + D(p_{4}). $$

You have the conservation constraint,

$$p_{1} + p_{2} = p_{3} + p_{4}, $$

and the on-shell constraints,

$$ \left\Vert p_{1} \right\Vert^{2} + m_{1}^{2} = 0, \qquad \left\Vert p_{2} \right\Vert^{2} + m_{2}^{2} = 0, \qquad \left\Vert p_{3} \right\Vert^{2} + m_{3}^{2} = 0, \qquad \left\Vert p_{4} \right\Vert^{2} + m_{4}^{2} = 0. $$

Traditionally, you use the Mandelstam invariants to describe the kinematics of this process:

$$ s = -\left\Vert p_{1} + p_{2} \right\Vert^{2} = -\left\Vert p_{3} + p_{4} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad t = -\left\Vert p_{1} - p_{3} \right\Vert^{2} = -\left\Vert p_{4} - p_{2} \right\Vert^{2} , \qquad u = -\left\Vert p_{1} - p_{4} \right\Vert^{2} = -\left\Vert p_{3} - p_{2} \right\Vert^{2}. $$

Due to the conservation constraint, the three Mandelstam invariants satisfy a linear constraint:

$$ s + t + u = m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2} + m_{3}^{2} + m_{4}^{2}. $$

I will refer to the process of writing all physically-relevant quantities (e.g. energy, spatial momentum, speed, rapidity, scattering angles) in terms of the masses and the Mandelstam invariants as the Mandelstam kinematic analysis.

What I call the Sudakov kinematic analysis consist of decomposing the incoming/outgoing slow momenta into incoming/outgoing null momenta. That is, let \(k_{1}\), \(k_{2}\), \(k_{3}\), and \(k_{4}\) be null momenta and consider

$$ p_{1} = k_{1} - \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{1} \cdot k_{2} \right)} k_{2}, \qquad p_{2} = k_{2} - \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{1} \cdot k_{2} \right)} k_{1}, \qquad p_{3} = k_{3} - \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{3} \cdot k_{4} \right)} k_{4}, \qquad p_{4} = k_{4} - \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{3} \cdot k_{4} \right)} k_{3}. $$

This is valid because, in general, a linear combination of two null vectors is not null. The conservation constraint becomes

$$ \left[ 1 - \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{1} \cdot k_{2} \right)} \right] k_{1} + \left[ 1 - \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{1} \cdot k_{2} \right)} \right] k_{2} = \left[ 1 - \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{3} \cdot k_{4} \right)} \right] k_{3} + \left[ 1 - \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{2 \left( k_{3} \cdot k_{4} \right)} \right] k_{4}. $$

You can introduce six Sudakov invariants:

$$ S_{12} = - \left\Vert k_{1} + k_{2} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad T_{13} = -\left\Vert k_{1} - k_{3} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad U_{14} = -\left\Vert k_{1} - k_{4} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad U_{23} = -\left\Vert k_{3} - k_{2} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad T_{24} = -\left\Vert k_{4} - k_{2} \right\Vert^{2}, \qquad S_{34} = -\left\Vert k_{3} + k_{4} \right\Vert^{2}.$$

In principle, you can write these invariants in terms of the masses and the Mandelstam invariants. The system of six equations to solve is

$$ s = \left( 1 + \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) \left( 1 + \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) S_{12}, \qquad s = \left( 1 + \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) \left( 1 + \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) S_{34}; $$
$$ t = m_{1}^{2} + m_{3}^{2} + T_{13} + \left( \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) U_{14} + \left( \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) U_{23} + \left( \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{3}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \right) T_{24}, \qquad t = m_{2}^{2} + m_{4}^{2} + T_{24} + \left( \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) U_{23} + \left( \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) U_{14} + \left( \frac{m_{2}^{2} m_{4}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \right) T_{13};$$
$$ u = m_{1}^{2} + m_{4}^{2} + U_{14} + \left( \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) T_{13} + \left( \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) T_{24} + \left( \frac{ m_{1}^{2} m_{4}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \right) U_{23}, \qquad u = m_{2}^{2} + m_{3}^{2} + U_{23} + \left( \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{S_{34}} \right) T_{24} + \left( \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} \right) T_{13} + \left( \frac{m_{2}^{2} m_{3}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \right) U_{14}.$$

The solution is

$$ S_{12} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left[ s - \left( m_{1} - m_{2} \right)^{2} \right] \left[ s - \left( m_{1} + m_{2} \right)^{2} \right] } \right);$$
$$ S_{34} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s - m_{3}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left[ s - \left( m_{3} - m_{4} \right)^{2} \right] \left[ s - \left( m_{3} + m_{4} \right)^{2} \right] } \right);$$
$$ T_{13} = \frac{S_{12}^{2} S_{34}^{2}}{\left(S_{12}^{2} - m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \right) \left(S_{34}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} m_{4}^{2} \right)} \left[\left( t - m_{1}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{S_{34}} \left( u - m_{1}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} \left( u - m_{2}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) + \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{3}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \left( t - m_{2}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) \right] ;$$
$$ T_{24} = \frac{S_{12}^{2} S_{34}^{2}}{\left(S_{12}^{2} - m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \right) \left(S_{34}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} m_{4}^{2} \right)} \left[ \left( t - m_{2}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{S_{34}} \left( u - m_{2}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} \left( u - m_{1}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) + \frac{m_{2}^{2} m_{4}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \left( t - m_{1}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) \right] ;$$
$$ U_{14} = \frac{S_{12}^{2} S_{34}^{2}}{\left(S_{12}^{2} - m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \right) \left(S_{34}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} m_{4}^{2} \right)} \left[ \left( u - m_{1}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{4}^{2}}{S_{34}} \left( t - m_{1}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} \left( t - m_{2}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) + \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{4}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \left( u - m_{2}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) \right] ;$$
$$ U_{23} = \frac{S_{12}^{2} S_{34}^{2}}{\left(S_{12}^{2} - m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \right) \left(S_{34}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} m_{4}^{2} \right)} \left[ \left( u - m_{2}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{3}^{2}}{S_{34}} \left( t - m_{2}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) - \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} \left( t - m_{1}^{2} - m_{3}^{2} \right) + \frac{m_{2}^{2} m_{3}^{2}}{S_{12} S_{34}} \left( u - m_{1}^{2} - m_{4}^{2} \right) \right] .$$

Note that in four spacetime dimensions all Sudakov invariants can be written in terms of twistor products.
