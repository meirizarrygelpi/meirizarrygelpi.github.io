Two-Quantum Sudakov Decomposition

Date Wed 25 November 2015 By M.E. Irizarry-Gelpí Category Physics Tags sudakov

In this post I will consider a problem with two independent momentum vectors, each of which describes a distinct massive quantum. That is, you do not have any conservation constraint relating these two momentum vectors. But you still have two on-shell constraints:

$$ p_{1}^{2} = m_{1}^{2}, \qquad p_{2}^{2} = m_{2}^{2}.$$

You can introduce a 2-Mandelstam invariant:

$$ s_{12} \equiv \left( p_{1} + p_{2} \right)^{2}.$$

You can also introduce the corresponding Regge-Mandelstam invariant:

$$ r_{12} \equiv \frac{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2}}{2 m_{1} m_{2}}.$$

There is one 2-Gram invariant:

$$ G_{12} \equiv - \det{ \begin{pmatrix} p_{1}^{2} & p_{1} \cdot p_{2} \\ p_{2} \cdot p_{1} & p_{2}^{2} \end{pmatrix} } = \frac{1}{4} \left[ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \right] = m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \left( r_{12}^{2} - 1 \right).$$

All of these invariants are present in any two-quantum problem.

The two-quantum Sudakov decomposition is given by

$$ p_{1} = n_{1} + \frac{m_{1}^{2}}{S_{12}} n_{2}, \qquad p_{2} = n_{2} + \frac{m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} n_{1}.$$

Here \(n_{1}\) and \(n_{2}\) are null vectors, and I have introduced a 2-Sudakov invariant:

$$ S_{12} \equiv \left( n_{1} + n_{2} \right)^{2} = 2 \left( n_{1} \cdot n_{2} \right).$$

This decomposition can be thought of as a way to explicitly solve the on-shell constraints. The goal now is to write the 2-Sudakov invariant \(S_{12}\) in terms of the Mandelstam invariant \(s_{12}\) and the masses. In order to do this, it is helpful to also introduce a Regge-Sudakov invariant:

$$ R_{12} \equiv \frac{S_{12}}{m_{1} m_{2}}.$$

Using the identity

$$ r_{12} = \frac{p_{1} \cdot p_{2}}{m_{1} m_{2}},$$

you find the relation

$$ 2r_{12} = R_{12} + \frac{1}{R_{12}}.$$

Solving this gives

$$ R_{12} = r_{12} + \sqrt{r_{12}^{2} - 1}.$$

Thus,

$$ S_{12} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right).$$

Note that in order for \(S_{12}\) to be real, you need

$$ r_{12} \leq -1 \text{ or } r_{12} \geq 1.$$

That is,

$$ s_{12} \leq \left( m_{1} - m_{2} \right)^{2} \text{ or } s_{12} \geq \left( m_{1} + m_{2} \right)^{2}.$$

This is equivalent to \(G_{12} \geq 0\).

The following relations are useful:

$$ S_{12} + m_{1}^{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} + m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right), \qquad S_{12} + m_{2}^{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right);$$
$$ \frac{\left(S_{12} + m_{1}^{2} \right) \left(S_{12} + m_{2}^{2} \right)}{S_{12}} = s_{12} ;$$
$$ S_{12} = \frac{2 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }};$$
$$ \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right);$$
$$ \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} + m_{1}^{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} + m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right), \qquad \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} + m_{2}^{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} } \right);$$
$$ S_{12} + \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} = s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2}, \qquad S_{12} - \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} = \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} };$$
$$ S_{12} + 2 m_{1}^{2} + \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} = s_{12} + m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2}, \qquad S_{12} + 2 m_{2}^{2} + \frac{m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2}}{S_{12}} = s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2};$$
$$ \frac{S_{12} \left( S_{12} + m_{1}^{2} \right)}{m_{1}^{2} \left( S_{12} + m_{2}^{2} \right)} = \frac{s_{12} + m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }}{s_{12} + m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }};$$
$$ \frac{S_{12} \left( S_{12} + m_{2}^{2} \right)}{m_{2}^{2} \left( S_{12} + m_{1}^{2} \right)} = \frac{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }}{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} + m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }};$$
$$ S_{12}^{2} = m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \left( \frac{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} + \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }}{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }} \right) \quad \Longrightarrow \quad R_{12}^{2} = \frac{r_{12} + \sqrt{ r_{12}^{2} - 1 }}{r_{12} - \sqrt{ r_{12}^{2} - 1 }};$$
$$ S_{12}^{2} + m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} = 2m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \left( \frac{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2}}{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }} \right) \quad \Longrightarrow \quad R_{12}^{2} + 1 = \frac{2 r_{12}}{r_{12} - \sqrt{ r_{12}^{2} - 1 }};$$
$$ S_{12}^{2} - m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} = 2m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} \left( \frac{\sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }}{s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} - \sqrt{ \left( s_{12} - m_{1}^{2} - m_{2}^{2} \right)^{2} - 4 m_{1}^{2} m_{2}^{2} }} \right) \quad \Longrightarrow \quad R_{12}^{2} - 1 = \frac{2 \sqrt{r_{12}^{2} - 1}}{r_{12} - \sqrt{ r_{12}^{2} - 1 }}.$$

The logarithm of some of these expressions appear as rapidities in the center-of-momentum frame for either a 2-to-\(N\) or an \(N\)-to-2 scattering problem.
